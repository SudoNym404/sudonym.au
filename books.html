Why this page exists

A lot of what I’m doing is hands-on, but books and structured resources still help fill the gaps, especially when I want a clearer explanation of how something works rather than just following steps.

This page gives me a place to track the material that’s helped most, and later it can become a useful reference for anyone following a similar path into IT.

Reading Notes

A simple area for takeaways, thoughts, and points I want to remember while working through books and study material.

Concepts make more sense when tied to lab work

Reading helps most when I can connect it to something I’ve configured, broken, fixed, or tested myself. That tends to make the theory stick much better.

Good references save time later

Even when I do not remember every detail, having solid reference material makes it easier to go back, verify something properly, and build better habits over time.

Some topics need repeat exposure

Areas like subnetting, routing behaviour, Linux services, and cloud architecture often click better after reading them more than once and seeing them in different forms.
